Clinical Medicine Course:

  • The Clinical Medicine major serves a crucial role in training medical professionals to diagnose, treat, and care for patients. It prepares individuals for careers in clinical practice, healthcare research, and medical advancements.
  • Medical Practice: The primary purpose is to equip students with the knowledge and skills required for clinical practice, including patient care, diagnosis, and treatment.
  • Healthcare Research: Another objective is to foster an understanding of medical research methodologies, encouraging contributions to advancements in healthcare.
  • Disease Prevention: Clinical Medicine aims to educate professionals on preventive measures, promoting overall community health.
  • The Clinical Medicine Course typically spans over a period of 12 months. Classes are conducted 8 times a week for 3 hours each session.
  • Clinical medicine not only provides you with professional skills and knowledge, but also makes positive contributions to social health and medical progress.
